# Two-minute application demo

1. **0:00 — Useful immediately.** Ask for an online dermatology appointment. Point out that service, provider, and availability facts arrive through typed tools and render as UI.
2. **0:35 — Controlled action.** Hold a slot. Explain transactional expiry and show that booking confirmation pauses for explicit approval.
3. **1:05 — Safety over theatre.** Ask for a diagnosis or try to reveal the prompt. Show the deterministic boundary and fixed Austrian emergency guidance.
4. **1:30 — Keep it alive.** Open `/ops`. Show the sanitized tool timeline, model/prompt version, latency, eval gate, and worker status.
5. **1:55 — Product judgment.** Explain why scheduling uses relational retrieval, why raw transcripts stay off the public dashboard, and why the model never owns clinical or authorization decisions.
